# Source Strategy

Use this reference when you need to decide where to search, how to search, and how to validate the results.

## Source Priority

Use this order by default:

1. Primary sources
2. Reputable wire services and top-tier business media
3. Specialist trade media
4. Expert commentary or analyst interpretation

Do not build the top findings from tier 4 alone.

## Primary-Source Categories

### China

- Ministries, commissions, and regulators
- National and local government portals
- Stock exchange announcements and listed-company filings
- Company newsrooms and investor relations pages
- Industry associations and standard-setting bodies
- Official statistics and customs data

### International

- National regulators and government departments
- Company filings, earnings releases, and investor relations pages
- Exchange disclosures
- Multilateral organizations and official statistical agencies
- Industry associations, standards bodies, and major conference announcements when official

## Secondary-Source Categories

Use these to expand coverage, compare framing, and surface developments that need follow-up.

- Global business wires and mainstream financial media
- High-quality Chinese business and technology media
- Specialist trade publications for the target industry
- Research houses only when they add clearly attributable data or framing

## Search Patterns

Search in both Chinese and English. Mix the industry term with one or more decision lenses.

### Chinese query patterns

- `<行业> 最新 资讯 政策`
- `<行业> 最新 融资 并购`
- `<行业> 头部企业 动态`
- `<行业> 出海 海外 市场 最新`
- `<行业> 供应链 价格 产能`
- `<公司名> 公告 财报 新闻稿`

### English query patterns

- `<industry> latest news regulation`
- `<industry> funding M&A latest`
- `<industry> supply chain pricing update`
- `<industry> major players latest`
- `<industry> commercialization milestone`
- `<company> investor relations earnings release`

## Validation Rules

- Anchor important developments to an exact publication or event date.
- Confirm major claims with at least two independent sources unless an official primary source already settles the point.
- When secondary reporting conflicts, surface the disagreement instead of forcing a false conclusion.
- Check whether a page is reporting the event or merely summarizing another article.

## Boss-Relevance Filter

Promote an item into the final briefing only if it likely affects one or more of:

- revenue growth
- profitability or cost structure
- market access
- valuation or financing conditions
- product roadmap or technology timing
- supply continuity
- competitive position
- compliance risk

## Red Flags

De-prioritize or exclude:

- undated summaries
- copied aggregator content
- clickbait headlines without evidence
- rumor-only social posts
- generic trend articles with no recent trigger
